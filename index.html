Benefits of Cloth Diapers

It is estimated that by the age of 2 1/2, a baby will spend 22,000 hours in diapers and parents will have changed over 13,000 diapers!

Disposable diapers are the third largest single consumer item in landfills, and represent 30% of non-biodegradable waste.

Cotton breathes, letting air circulate to baby's skin. And, unlike chemical-laden disposables, cotton diapers are naturally absorbent. It also means less friction on tender skin, reducing the likelihood of diaper rash.

Studies show that a child in cloth diapers will potty train 6 months to a year earlier than a child in single-use diapers, thereby saving your family hundreds of dollars! Therefore, on average, cloth diapering will be less costly than single-use diapers.
